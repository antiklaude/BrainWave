## Phase 1 — Learn (40 min)

**Why this matters:** Color is the first thing people notice and the last thing they can articulate. A wrong color choice makes a brand feel untrustworthy, cheap, or confusing — even to people who can't explain why. Color theory gives you a framework to make intentional decisions instead of guessing.

- **Color wheel** — primary colors (red, yellow, blue) → mix to make secondary (orange, green, purple) → mix those for tertiary. The wheel shows relationships.
- **Hue / Saturation / Value (HSB)** — Hue is the color identity (red, blue, green). Saturation is the vividness (0% = grey, 100% = pure). Value/Brightness is how light or dark (0% = black, 100% = full brightness).
- **Warm vs cool** — reds, oranges, yellows feel warm and energetic. Blues, greens, purples feel cool and calm. This isn't decoration — it's psychology.
- **Color schemes**:
  - *Monochromatic* — one hue, varied saturation/value. Safe, elegant, easy.
  - *Analogous* — three adjacent hues. Harmonious, natural.
  - *Complementary* — two opposite hues. High contrast, vibrant, can be overwhelming if used equally.
  - *Triadic* — three equally spaced hues. Energetic, balanced.
- **Color psychology** — Blue signals trust and reliability (banking, healthcare, tech). Red signals urgency, energy, passion (retail sales, food). Green signals growth, nature, health. Yellow signals optimism and warmth. **Important:** these are cultural associations, not universal laws. They shift by context and audience.

> **Key insight:** In a professional palette, you usually need: one dominant brand color (60%), one supporting color (30%), and one accent for calls to action (10%). More than three colors in a UI creates visual noise.

## Phase 2 — Do (40 min)

- Choose one of these brand scenarios: a specialty coffee shop, an AI technology startup, or a children's health clinic
- Pick a 3-color palette (dominant, supporting, accent) from a color palette tool like coolors.co
- For each color, write one sentence explaining why it fits the brand: connect it to color psychology, the target audience, and the emotional tone
- Try the complementary scheme for your dominant color — does it work or clash? Why?

## Review

What is the difference between saturation and brightness? Can you pick a color that is high-brightness but low-saturation? What does it look like?
