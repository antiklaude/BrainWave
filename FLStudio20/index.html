⚡

The 20/80 Rule

80% of finished tracks use: a basic beat, one melody, bass, reverb + delay, and basic mixing. Master these before touching advanced concepts.

🎯

Project-First Learning

Every session ends with something you can hit play on. No theory-only sessions. You learn by doing, not watching tutorials for hours.

🔄

Genre Rotation

Start with Trap and LoFi (slower tempos, fewer elements). EDM and Trance come mid-plan. Ambient unlocks creative freedom once fundamentals are solid.

📝

15-Min Review Ritual

End every session: play your project back in full, write one thing you nailed, one thing that sounds off, and set your next session's starting goal.

Trap LoFi EDM Trance Ambient
▶ LVL 1 Orientation — survive FL Studio Milestone: a 4-bar beat plays
  • FL Studio layout: Channel Rack, Piano Roll, Playlist, Mixer
  • Step Sequencer: build a 4-bar drum pattern
  • BPM and time signature basics
  • Load and play a stock sample or preset
  • Export a WAV — your first file to show
▶ LVL 2 Core ingredients — beat + melody + bass Milestone: a 1-min loop with 3 elements
  • Piano Roll: drawing and editing MIDI notes
  • Choosing the right scale (minor = trap/LoFi, major = EDM)
  • Bass fundamentals: sidechaining to kick
  • Velocity and note length for human feel
  • Basic synth: Serum or 3xOsc, one patch from scratch
▶ LVL 3 Structure — full tracks, not just loops Milestone: 2–3 min track with intro, drop, outro
  • Playlist view: arrangement from loop to track
  • Energy flow: buildup, drop, breakdown structure
  • Transitions: risers, sweeps, cymbal swells
  • Automation: filter sweeps, volume rides
  • Layering: 2–3 synths without mudding the mix
▶ LVL 4 Mixing — your track sounds clean Milestone: plays well on phones + headphones
  • FL Mixer: routing channels, gain staging
  • EQ: cutting mud (low-end) and harsh frequencies
  • Compression: punchy drums, controlled bass
  • Reverb + Delay: space without washing out
  • Stereo width and panning fundamentals
▶ LVL 5 Genre mastery — signature sounds per genre Milestone: one track in each target genre
  • Trap: 808 design, hi-hat rolls, pitch automation
  • LoFi: vinyl crackle, tape saturation, jazz chords
  • EDM: supersaw lead, pumping sidechain, large reverb drops
  • Trance: arpeggiated leads, 138 BPM, long buildups
  • Ambient: drone pads, granular textures, slow evolution

Phase 1 — Learn (40 min)
→Bass 101: bass note = root note of your chord/scale (C for C minor).

→808 bass: long sustained note, pitch-slides — signature of trap.

→Set up basic sidechain: kick triggers bass volume to duck.
→Add bass to sessions 1+2 project. You now have 3 elements.
15-Min Review

Turn the bass way up. Does it clash with the kick, or complement it? The kick and bass should feel like one punch, not two separate sounds.

Phase 1 — Learn (45 min)
→FL Mixer: route each instrument to its own channel.
→EQ: cut below 40Hz on everything except bass/kick. Cut 200–400Hz mud.
→Compression on drums: attack 10ms, release 60ms, ratio 4:1.
Phase 2 — Do (60 min)
→Open your LoFi track. Route all instruments to the mixer.
→Apply EQ cuts to kick, snare, melody. Listen before and after.
→Add light compression to the drum bus. Compare bypass vs active.
15-Min Review

Solo each instrument. Does anything sound harsh, boxy, or muddy on its own? A good mix starts with each element sounding clean in isolation.

Reverb & Delay in FL Studio

Phase 1 — Learn (40 min)
→Reverb adds size. Short for drums, longer for pads and synths.
→Delay: rhythmic echo. Quarter-note delay synced to BPM.
→Rule: if reverb sounds good at 100%, the real setting is 30%.
→Send/Return routing in FL Mixer for shared reverb bus.
Phase 2 — Do (65 min)
→Add Fruity Reeverb 2 to melody send channel. Decay 1.5s.
→Add quarter-note delay to a hi-hat or synth stab.
→Build an ambient pad: any synth + heavy reverb + slow attack.
15-Min Review

Solo the wet reverb channel. If you clearly hear the melody through the reverb tail alone, it's too loud. Reverb should feel like air around the note, not a second instrument.

Phase 1 — Learn (35 min)
→Trap tempo: 140 BPM. 808 note: long, pitch-slides using portamento.
→Hi-hat rolls: 1/32 triplets, velocity randomized (not all the same volume).
→Automation clip: pitch-bend the 808 down at end of each bar.
Phase 2 — Do (70 min)
→New project. Kick on 1, snare on 3, rolling hi-hats.
→Add 808 bass with pitch automation. Let it slide and growl.
→Add one dark minor melody. Arrange to 2 min. Export.
15-Min Review

Does the 808 hit you in the chest? If you can't feel it on speakers, it needs more low-end. Does it clash with the melody? Tune the 808 root note to match your key.

Phase 1 — Learn (40 min)
→EDM tempo: 128 BPM. Supersaw = multiple detuned oscillators stacked.
→Build a supersaw in Serum or 3xOsc: 3 unison voices, slight detune, stereo spread.
→Drop architecture: filter closes over 8 bars → fill → 1-beat silence → drop.
Phase 2 — Do (65 min)
→128 BPM. 4/4 kick on every beat. Clap on 2 and 4.
→4-chord progression + supersaw lead on top.
→Build the drop using filter cutoff automation. Filter fully opens = the drop.
→Arrange: 8 bar buildup → 16 bar drop → repeat. Export.
15-Min Review

Play to the drop. Did you feel tension building? The moment the drop hits should feel like a release of pressure. If not, your buildup needs more filter movement and harder fills.

Phase 1 — Learn (40 min)
→Trance: 138 BPM, arpeggio lead through a minor scale, long reverb on everything.
→FL Arpeggiator: set to 1/16, minor scale lock, 2 octaves.
→Ambient: no drums needed. Pads with 2–4s attack, massive reverb, slow LFO on filter.
Phase 2 — Do (65 min)
→Part A (45 min): Trance — 138 BPM, arpeggio lead, kick+bass, 2-min arrangement.
→Part B (20 min): Ambient — one pad sound, huge reverb, slow filter automation. No drums.
→Export both. You now have 5 genre sketches.
15-Min Review

Listen to the ambient piece with your eyes closed. Does it feel like a space? Good ambient evolves slowly. If nothing changes in the first 30 seconds, add subtle filter automation.

Phase 1 — Choose + Fix (60 min)
→Listen to all tracks from sessions 4, 7, 8, 9 back to back.
→Pick your strongest. Open it. Fix the top 3 things that bother you.
→Check: EQ on all channels, reverb balance, automation that builds energy.
Phase 2 — Master + Publish (55 min)
→Add Fruity Loudness Maximizer on master channel. Set ceiling to -1dB LUFS.
→A/B test: your mix vs a reference track in the same genre.
→Export final WAV. Upload to SoundCloud or a producer Discord for feedback.
Final Retrospective — 15 Min

Compare your Session 1 beat to your Session 10 track. List 5 things you now know how to do that you didn't at the start. That list is your proof of progress. Set 3 goals for the next 20 hours.
